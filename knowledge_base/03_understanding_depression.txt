UNDERSTANDING DEPRESSION

WHAT IS DEPRESSION?
Depression (Major Depressive Disorder) is more than feeling sad or having a bad day. It's a serious mental health condition that affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities.

COMMON SYMPTOMS:
Emotional:
- Persistent sad, anxious, or "empty" mood
- Feelings of hopelessness or pessimism
- Loss of interest in activities once enjoyed
- Feelings of guilt or worthlessness
- Irritability or frustration

Physical:
- Changes in appetite or weight
- Sleep problems (insomnia or oversleeping)
- Fatigue and decreased energy
- Physical aches and pains
- Slowed movements or speech

Cognitive:
- Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
- Memory problems
- Thoughts of death or suicide
- Difficulty thinking clearly

TYPES OF DEPRESSION:
- Major Depressive Disorder: Severe symptoms that interfere with daily life
- Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia): Long-term, chronic depression
- Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD): Depression related to seasons
- Postpartum Depression: Occurs after childbirth
- Bipolar Disorder: Alternating between depression and mania

WHAT CAUSES DEPRESSION?
- Biological factors (brain chemistry, genetics)
- Life events (trauma, loss, stress)
- Medical conditions
- Substance use
- Combination of factors

Often, there's no single cause - it's complex and different for everyone.

WHAT HELPS WITH DEPRESSION:

Professional Treatment:
- Psychotherapy (especially CBT and interpersonal therapy)
- Medication (antidepressants prescribed by a doctor)
- Combination of therapy and medication often most effective
- In severe cases: hospitalization or specialized programs

Self-Care Strategies:
- Maintain a routine and structure
- Exercise regularly (even small amounts help)
- Get adequate sleep (but not too much)
- Eat nutritious meals
- Limit alcohol and avoid drugs
- Stay connected with supportive people
- Break large tasks into smaller steps
- Be patient with yourself
- Avoid isolation
- Do small activities that once brought joy

IMPORTANT TO KNOW:
- Depression is NOT a weakness or character flaw
- It's a medical condition with effective treatments
- Recovery takes time - be patient
- Most people improve with proper treatment
- Relapses can happen - that's okay, keep going
- You deserve help and support

WHEN TO SEEK HELP:
- Symptoms persist for more than two weeks
- Depression interferes with work, school, or relationships
- You have thoughts of self-harm or suicide (call 988 immediately)
- Self-care strategies aren't helping
- You're experiencing severe symptoms

SUPPORTING SOMEONE WITH DEPRESSION:
- Listen without judgment
- Offer practical help
- Encourage professional treatment
- Be patient and supportive
- Take care of yourself too

Remember: Depression is treatable. With proper support and treatment, people can and do recover.

This information is educational and not a substitute for professional medical advice. Please consult a licensed mental health professional for personalized care.
