COPING STRATEGIES AND TECHNIQUES

BREATHING EXERCISES

4-7-8 Breathing:
1. Exhale completely through your mouth
2. Inhale through nose for 4 counts
3. Hold breath for 7 counts
4. Exhale through mouth for 8 counts
5. Repeat 3-4 times

Box Breathing:
1. Inhale for 4 counts
2. Hold for 4 counts
3. Exhale for 4 counts
4. Hold for 4 counts
5. Repeat several times

Belly Breathing:
1. Place one hand on chest, one on belly
2. Breathe slowly through nose into belly
3. Belly should rise more than chest
4. Exhale slowly through mouth
5. Continue for 5-10 minutes

GROUNDING TECHNIQUES

5-4-3-2-1 Method:
- Name 5 things you can see
- Name 4 things you can touch
- Name 3 things you can hear
- Name 2 things you can smell
- Name 1 thing you can taste

Physical Grounding:
- Hold ice cubes or splash cold water on face
- Press feet firmly on the ground
- Touch different textures around you
- Do jumping jacks or physical movement
- Focus on physical sensations

Mental Grounding:
- Count backwards from 100 by 7s
- Name all items of one color you can see
- Recite something you've memorized
- Describe your surroundings in detail
- Think of a favorite place in detail

MINDFULNESS PRACTICES

Body Scan:
1. Lie down comfortably
2. Focus attention on each body part
3. Start at toes, move up to head
4. Notice sensations without judgment
5. Release tension as you go

Mindful Observation:
1. Choose an object (flower, art, etc.)
2. Look at it as if seeing it for the first time
3. Notice colors, textures, shapes
4. Focus fully on the object
5. Let other thoughts pass by

Mindful Walking:
1. Walk slowly and deliberately
2. Notice each step and movement
3. Feel your feet touching the ground
4. Observe your surroundings
5. Return attention when mind wanders

PROGRESSIVE MUSCLE RELAXATION
1. Find a quiet, comfortable space
2. Tense each muscle group for 5 seconds
3. Release and notice the relaxation
4. Start with feet, work up to head
5. Breathe deeply throughout

COGNITIVE TECHNIQUES

Thought Challenging:
- Identify the negative thought
- Ask: Is this thought realistic?
- Look for evidence for and against it
- Consider alternative perspectives
- Replace with balanced thought

Worry Time:
- Set aside 15 minutes daily for worrying
- When worries arise, write them down
- Address them during designated time
- Outside worry time, redirect thoughts

Positive Self-Talk:
- Replace "I can't" with "I'll try"
- Use "I am learning" instead of "I failed"
- Practice self-compassion
- Speak to yourself as you would a friend

HEALTHY DISTRACTIONS

When you need a break from difficult emotions:
- Call or text a friend
- Watch a favorite show or movie
- Listen to uplifting music
- Engage in a hobby
- Do a puzzle or game
- Read a book
- Take a warm bath
- Go for a walk
- Do creative activities (draw, write, cook)
- Play with a pet

EMOTIONAL REGULATION

When feeling overwhelmed:
- Name your emotion ("I feel anxious")
- Accept it without judgment
- Remind yourself it's temporary
- Use coping strategies
- Reach out for support if needed

Creating a Calm Space:
- Designate a comfortable area
- Add calming elements (soft lighting, blankets)
- Keep comfort items nearby
- Use for relaxation and self-care

BUILDING RESILIENCE

Daily Practices:
- Maintain regular sleep schedule
- Exercise regularly (even 10 minutes helps)
- Eat balanced, nutritious meals
- Stay connected with supportive people
- Set realistic goals and expectations
- Practice gratitude (3 things daily)
- Limit news and social media
- Make time for activities you enjoy

WHEN COPING ISN'T ENOUGH

These strategies are helpful tools, but professional support is important when:
- Symptoms persist or worsen
- Daily functioning is impaired
- You feel unsafe
- Self-help isn't providing relief

Remember: Using coping strategies takes practice. Be patient with yourself as you learn what works best for you.

This information is for educational purposes. For personalized mental health support, please consult with a licensed professional.
