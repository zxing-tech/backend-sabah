CRISIS RESOURCES AND EMERGENCY SUPPORT

IMMEDIATE HELP - CALL NOW IF YOU'RE IN CRISIS:
- National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988 (US)
- Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741 (US)
- International Association for Suicide Prevention: https://www.iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres/

WHEN TO SEEK IMMEDIATE HELP:
- Thoughts of harming yourself or others
- Severe panic or anxiety that feels unmanageable
- Sudden changes in behavior or mood
- Hearing voices or experiencing hallucinations
- Feeling completely hopeless or trapped
- Recent trauma or abuse

24/7 SUPPORT LINES:
- SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357 (mental health and substance abuse)
- National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233
- Veterans Crisis Line: 988, then press 1
- LGBTQ+ Youth Support (Trevor Project): 1-866-488-7386
- Disaster Distress Helpline: 1-800-985-5990

WHAT TO EXPECT WHEN YOU CALL:
- Trained counselors who listen without judgment
- Confidential support and resources
- Help creating a safety plan
- Referrals to local services
- Crisis intervention if needed

REMEMBER:
- Asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness
- Crisis feelings are temporary - they will pass
- You deserve support and care
- Many people have felt this way and found help
- Recovery is possible

This information is for educational purposes only and does not replace professional medical advice.
