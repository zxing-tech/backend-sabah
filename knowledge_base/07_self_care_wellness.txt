SELF-CARE AND WELLNESS

WHAT IS SELF-CARE?

Self-care means taking deliberate actions to maintain and improve your physical, mental, and emotional health. It's not selfish - it's necessary for well-being and helps you better support others.

Self-care is NOT:
- An indulgence or luxury
- Being selfish or self-centered
- Something you do only when things are bad
- A one-time activity
- The same for everyone

Self-care IS:
- A necessary part of health
- Preventive maintenance for well-being
- Something to practice regularly
- Personalized to your needs
- About making time for yourself

DIMENSIONS OF SELF-CARE

PHYSICAL SELF-CARE

Sleep:
- Aim for 7-9 hours nightly
- Keep consistent sleep schedule
- Create calming bedtime routine
- Limit screens before bed
- Keep bedroom cool, dark, quiet
- Avoid caffeine late in day

Exercise:
- Find activities you enjoy
- Aim for 30 minutes most days
- Include strength, cardio, flexibility
- Walk, dance, swim, cycle - anything that moves your body
- Start small and build gradually
- Movement is better than perfection

Nutrition:
- Eat balanced, varied diet
- Include fruits and vegetables
- Stay hydrated (water throughout day)
- Limit processed foods and sugar
- Don't skip meals
- Notice how foods affect your mood and energy
- Allow yourself treats in moderation

Health Maintenance:
- Attend regular check-ups
- Take prescribed medications
- Address health concerns promptly
- Practice good hygiene
- Protect yourself from illness
- Listen to your body's signals

EMOTIONAL SELF-CARE

Acknowledge Feelings:
- Name your emotions
- Allow yourself to feel without judgment
- Express emotions in healthy ways
- Journal about your feelings
- Talk to trusted people
- Remember: all feelings are valid

Set Boundaries:
- Learn to say no
- Limit toxic relationships
- Protect your time and energy
- Communicate your needs clearly
- Don't overcommit
- It's okay to disappoint others sometimes

Practice Self-Compassion:
- Speak kindly to yourself
- Forgive yourself for mistakes
- Treat yourself as you would a friend
- Accept imperfection
- Celebrate small wins
- Be patient with your progress

Stress Management:
- Use relaxation techniques daily
- Take breaks when overwhelmed
- Practice mindfulness
- Identify and manage triggers
- Ask for help when needed
- Engage in calming activities

MENTAL SELF-CARE

Stimulate Your Mind:
- Read books or articles
- Learn new skills or hobbies
- Solve puzzles or play brain games
- Engage in creative activities
- Limit negative news consumption
- Practice curiosity and wonder

Mindfulness Practices:
- Meditation (even 5 minutes daily)
- Deep breathing exercises
- Body scans
- Mindful eating or walking
- Focus on present moment
- Observe thoughts without judgment

Personal Growth:
- Set realistic goals
- Challenge yourself appropriately
- Reflect on your values
- Keep learning and growing
- Track progress and achievements
- Adjust goals as needed

SOCIAL SELF-CARE

Nurture Relationships:
- Spend time with supportive people
- Maintain meaningful connections
- Communicate openly and honestly
- Ask for and offer support
- Make time for friends and family
- Quality over quantity

Set Social Boundaries:
- Limit time with draining people
- Take breaks from social obligations
- Say no to events that deplete you
- Balance alone time and social time
- Protect your energy
- Choose relationships wisely

Community Connection:
- Join groups with shared interests
- Volunteer for causes you care about
- Participate in community activities
- Build sense of belonging
- Give and receive support
- Connect with like-minded people

SPIRITUAL SELF-CARE

(This is personal and different for everyone)

Find Meaning:
- Explore your values and beliefs
- Engage in practices that feel meaningful
- Connect with something larger than yourself
- Reflect on life's purpose
- Practice gratitude
- Seek inspiration

Spiritual Practices:
- Meditation or prayer
- Spend time in nature
- Creative expression
- Reading inspirational material
- Attending services or gatherings
- Contemplation and reflection
- Acts of kindness and service

PRACTICAL SELF-CARE

Create Routines:
- Morning routine to start day well
- Evening routine for better sleep
- Regular self-care practices
- Consistent meal and sleep times
- Build in buffer time
- Plan ahead when possible

Manage Your Environment:
- Keep living space reasonably organized
- Create calming spaces
- Reduce clutter gradually
- Add plants or natural elements
- Control noise levels
- Personalize your space

Time Management:
- Prioritize important tasks
- Break large projects into steps
- Use planners or calendars
- Schedule self-care time
- Build in breaks
- Be realistic about what you can do

Financial Self-Care:
- Create and follow a budget
- Save for emergencies
- Avoid comparison with others
- Seek help if struggling
- Make financial goals
- Practice mindful spending

DAILY SELF-CARE IDEAS

Morning:
- Stretch or do yoga
- Eat a nutritious breakfast
- Set intentions for the day
- Practice gratitude
- Avoid rushing
- Do something you enjoy

Throughout Day:
- Take regular breaks
- Drink water
- Step outside
- Practice deep breathing
- Move your body
- Check in with yourself

Evening:
- Disconnect from work
- Engage in relaxing activities
- Limit screen time before bed
- Prepare for tomorrow
- Reflect on the day
- Wind down with calming routine

SELF-CARE WHEN YOU'RE STRUGGLING

When feeling overwhelmed or low energy:
- Lower expectations temporarily
- Focus on basics (sleep, eating, hygiene)
- Do tiny acts of self-care
- Ask for help
- Be extra gentle with yourself
- Remember: it's temporary

Minimum Self-Care:
- Drink water
- Eat something
- Get some sleep
- Take shower/brush teeth
- Step outside briefly
- Reach out to one person

BUILDING SUSTAINABLE SELF-CARE

Start Small:
- Choose one area to focus on
- Make tiny changes first
- Build gradually over time
- Celebrate small wins
- Be consistent
- Adjust as needed

Make It Realistic:
- Fit self-care into your life
- Don't create more stress
- Simplify when needed
- Be flexible
- Do what actually helps you
- Abandon what doesn't work

Create Accountability:
- Schedule self-care like appointments
- Track your practices
- Tell someone your goals
- Join others in self-care
- Review and adjust regularly
- Be compassionate when you slip

OVERCOMING BARRIERS

"I don't have time"
- Start with 5 minutes
- Combine activities (walk while listening to music)
- Schedule it
- Self-care improves productivity
- It's an investment, not an expense

"I feel guilty"
- Self-care helps you help others
- You can't pour from empty cup
- It's necessary, not selfish
- You deserve care too
- Model healthy behavior for others

"I don't know what to do"
- Experiment and explore
- Notice what energizes you
- Ask others what they do
- Try different things
- Pay attention to your needs
- Start with basics

"It's not working"
- Give practices time to help
- Try different approaches
- Adjust to your needs
- Combine strategies
- Seek professional guidance
- Be patient with yourself

REMEMBER

- Self-care is not one-size-fits-all
- What works for others may not work for you
- It's okay to change your self-care practices
- Small, consistent actions add up
- You're worth the time and effort
- Self-care is a practice, not perfection
- Be patient and compassionate with yourself

This information is educational. For personalized wellness advice, consult with healthcare or mental health professionals.
