UNDERSTANDING ANXIETY

WHAT IS ANXIETY?
Anxiety is a natural response to stress or perceived threats. It becomes a concern when it's persistent, excessive, and interferes with daily life.

COMMON SYMPTOMS:
Physical:
- Rapid heartbeat or palpitations
- Sweating and trembling
- Shortness of breath
- Muscle tension
- Fatigue or restlessness
- Difficulty sleeping
- Digestive issues

Mental/Emotional:
- Persistent worry or fear
- Racing thoughts
- Difficulty concentrating
- Feeling on edge or irritable
- Sense of impending doom
- Avoiding certain situations

TYPES OF ANXIETY DISORDERS:
- Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD): Persistent worry about various topics
- Panic Disorder: Sudden, intense episodes of fear
- Social Anxiety: Fear of social situations and judgment
- Specific Phobias: Intense fear of particular objects or situations
- Health Anxiety: Excessive worry about health

WHAT HELPS WITH ANXIETY:

Immediate Relief:
- Deep breathing exercises (4-7-8 technique)
- Grounding techniques (5-4-3-2-1 method)
- Progressive muscle relaxation
- Gentle movement or walking
- Limiting caffeine and alcohol

Long-term Management:
- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
- Mindfulness and meditation practices
- Regular exercise (30 minutes most days)
- Adequate sleep (7-9 hours)
- Balanced nutrition
- Social support and connection
- Stress management techniques
- Medication (when prescribed by a doctor)

WHEN TO SEEK PROFESSIONAL HELP:
- Anxiety interferes with work, relationships, or daily activities
- Physical symptoms become overwhelming
- You're avoiding important life situations
- Self-help strategies aren't enough
- You're experiencing panic attacks
- Anxiety is affecting your quality of life

REMEMBER:
- Anxiety is very common - you're not alone
- It's treatable with the right support
- Many people manage anxiety successfully
- Small steps lead to big improvements
- Be patient and compassionate with yourself

For personalized advice and treatment, please consult with a licensed mental health professional.
